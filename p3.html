Conversational agent for Railway enquiry

Indian railway passengers can perform tasks like Book a ticket, Find trains etc., through Google assistant(Hindi) on mobile phone or kiosk on the platform instead of face-to-face enquiry.

"I think this is a good utility to convert an otherwise tedious task into a more easy, everyday activity, so yes I do like the idea of such an agent"

Abhishek

Passenger since 2012

Railways holds a complex set of information and processes which provides a confusion regarding planning a journey or about a booked journey. To assist passengers with their questions, Indian railways has setup a face-to-face enquiry system on the stations and also have different websites and mobile applications to disseminate the information. These mobile applications share parts of these information and help the passengers.

This project is an attempt to provide an accessible form of information dissemination instead of the face-to-face enquiry. The aim is to accommodate the passenger’s question with ease. For this conversation design is choosen in order to provide the enquiry in the form of conversation.

Autonomy


  • Mumbai CSMT station has 18 platforms and there are no self service systems on these platforms making the dependance to be on the enquiry counter at the station entry/exit.
  • Passengers in the enquiry queue have a sense of rushness because of others in the queue.
  • “Most of the enquiry questions are same.”
  • “The 5 important categories are the same and the way the questions are asked is different.”
  • “In CSMT 1 person sits for 12 hours staring at a computer screen and answering in the MIC.”
  • “Passengers questions irritate us a lot of time and they are repeated.”
  • “Railways already spends 50% of its expenditure on manpower.”
  • “But we are still underpaid.”

    • The current enquiry process clearly indicates a need for self service system.
